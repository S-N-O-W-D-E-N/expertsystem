What is Ringworm?

Tips for Managing Ringworm

Keep the Area Clean and Dry

Moisture can promote fungal growth, so keep the affected area clean and dry.

Avoid Scratching

Scratching can spread the infection to other areas of your body. Keep the area covered if necessary.

Disinfect Shared Surfaces

Ringworm is contagious. Disinfect common surfaces like towels, bedding, and gym equipment.

Treatment Options

Topical Antifungal Creams

Use over-the-counter or prescribed antifungal creams, lotions, or powders to treat the infection.

Oral Medications

For severe cases, your doctor may prescribe oral antifungal medications.

Home Remedies

Natural treatments such as tea tree oil or apple cider vinegar can sometimes help with ringworm.

General Advice

Wash Hands Regularly

To avoid spreading the infection, wash your hands thoroughly after touching the affected area.

Wear Loose, Breathable Clothing

Wear loose-fitting clothes made from breathable fabrics to reduce sweating and irritation.

Consult a Healthcare Provider

If the infection persists or worsens, seek advice from a healthcare professional for stronger treatments.
