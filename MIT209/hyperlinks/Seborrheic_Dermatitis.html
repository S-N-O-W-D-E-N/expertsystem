Seborrheic Dermatitis

Seborrheic dermatitis is a chronic skin condition characterized by red, scaly patches, and dandruff. It typically affects areas of the skin with a high density of sebaceous (oil) glands, such as the scalp, face, and upper chest.

The exact cause is unclear, but it may involve a combination of genetic, environmental, and fungal factors (such as Malassezia yeast). It is not contagious and often flares up during stressful periods or in cold weather.

  • Common Symptoms: Greasy, flaky skin; dandruff; redness; and itching in affected areas.
  • Common Locations: Scalp, eyebrows, sides of the nose, behind the ears, and upper chest.

Treatment includes regular cleansing, antifungal shampoos, topical corticosteroids, and moisturizing creams. Maintaining good skin hygiene and avoiding harsh irritants can help manage symptoms.
