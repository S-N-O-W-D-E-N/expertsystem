Stasis Dermatitis

Stasis dermatitis, also known as venous eczema, is a skin condition that occurs due to poor blood flow in the veins, usually in the lower legs. It often develops in individuals with chronic venous insufficiency or varicose veins.

As fluid builds up in the legs due to poor circulation, the skin becomes inflamed, discolored, and itchy. Over time, it can lead to open sores or ulcers if untreated.

  • Common Symptoms: Swelling in the lower legs, redness, itching, flaky skin, and brown discoloration.
  • Causes: Poor blood flow in the veins, varicose veins, or a history of blood clots.

Treatment involves improving circulation through elevation of the legs, wearing compression stockings, and using prescribed topical treatments for inflammation. In severe cases, medical procedures may be necessary to address underlying vein problems.
