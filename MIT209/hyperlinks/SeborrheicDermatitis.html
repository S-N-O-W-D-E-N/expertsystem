What is Seborrheic Dermatitis?

Tips for Managing Seborrheic Dermatitis

Shampoo Regularly

Use a gentle, anti-fungal or medicated shampoo to reduce dandruff and flakes, especially for scalp seborrheic dermatitis.

Moisturize Affected Areas

Apply a fragrance-free moisturizer to prevent the skin from becoming too dry and irritated.

Avoid Scratching

Try not to scratch affected areas, as this can worsen the condition and lead to infection.

Treatment Options

Topical Antifungals

Medications like ketoconazole or ciclopirox can be applied to affected areas to reduce fungal growth.

Topical Steroids

For inflammation, corticosteroid creams or ointments can be prescribed to reduce redness and irritation.

Medicated Shampoos

Shampoos containing selenium sulfide, zinc pyrithione, or coal tar can help control seborrheic dermatitis on the scalp.

General Advice

Identify and Avoid Triggers

Identify factors like stress, extreme weather, or certain skin products that may trigger flare-ups and try to avoid them.

Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eat foods rich in antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

Consult a Dermatologist

If symptoms persist or worsen, seek professional medical advice for tailored treatment plans.
