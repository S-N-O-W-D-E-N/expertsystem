Contact Dermatitis

Contact dermatitis is a type of skin irritation caused by direct contact with a substance or an allergic reaction to it. This condition can result in red, itchy, or inflamed skin and, in some cases, blistering or oozing.

There are two main types of contact dermatitis:

  • Irritant Contact Dermatitis: Caused by repeated exposure to irritating substances, such as cleaning products, soaps, or certain chemicals.
  • Allergic Contact Dermatitis: Triggered by an allergic reaction to substances such as nickel, fragrances, or certain plants like poison ivy.

Treatment typically involves identifying and avoiding the irritant or allergen, applying soothing creams, and, if needed, using prescribed medications like corticosteroids.
