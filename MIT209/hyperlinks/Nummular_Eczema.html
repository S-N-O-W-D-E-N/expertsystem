Nummular Eczema

Nummular eczema, also known as discoid eczema, is a chronic skin condition that causes coin-shaped patches of itchy, inflamed skin. These patches can appear anywhere on the body, often on the arms, legs, hands, or torso.

The exact cause of nummular eczema is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to dry skin, allergies, or irritation from environmental factors. It is more common in adults and tends to worsen during dry or cold weather.

  • Common Symptoms: Round, scaly patches of red or brown skin, intense itching, and occasional oozing or crusting.
  • Triggers: Dry skin, irritants like soap or detergents, and weather changes.

Treatment focuses on reducing symptoms and preventing flare-ups. This may include moisturizing the skin, using prescribed topical steroids, and avoiding known triggers.
