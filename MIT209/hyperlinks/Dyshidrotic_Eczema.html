Dyshidrotic Eczema

Dyshidrotic eczema, also known as pompholyx, is a skin condition that causes small, itchy blisters to form on the palms of the hands, soles of the feet, or sides of the fingers. These blisters can be painful and may crack or peel as they heal.

While the exact cause of dyshidrotic eczema is not known, it is often associated with factors such as stress, allergies, or exposure to irritants like metals or detergents. The condition tends to flare up in response to these triggers.

  • Common Symptoms: Intense itching, small fluid-filled blisters, and peeling or cracked skin.
  • Triggers: Stress, sweat, nickel or cobalt exposure, and seasonal allergies.

Treatment typically includes keeping the affected areas moisturized, avoiding known triggers, and using medicated creams or ointments prescribed by a healthcare provider.
