Symptoms of Skin Conditions

Symptoms are subjective indications of a potential medical condition, experienced and reported by an individual. They differ from signs, which are objective findings observable by others, such as a doctor during an examination. Symptoms provide vital clues to healthcare providers for diagnosing illnesses and can vary in severity, duration, and impact.

Skin Rash

Red, inflamed patches or spots on the skin, often itchy and may vary in size and intensity.

Dry Skin

Rough, scaly, or flaky skin that can be itchy and prone to cracking, especially in colder seasons.

Swelling

Localized or widespread swelling, often accompanied by redness or tenderness in the affected area.

Blisters

Small fluid-filled sacs on the skin, often caused by friction, burns, or infections.
