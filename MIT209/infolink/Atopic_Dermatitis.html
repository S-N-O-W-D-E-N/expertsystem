Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema)

Atopic dermatitis, commonly known as eczema, is a chronic skin condition characterized by inflammation, redness, and itching. It often appears in patches and can affect any part of the body, including the face, hands, and feet.

While the exact cause of eczema is not fully understood, it is believed to result from a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Common triggers include allergens, stress, weather changes, and irritants such as soaps or fabrics.

Managing eczema involves avoiding triggers, keeping the skin moisturized, and using prescribed treatments like topical corticosteroids or antihistamines.
