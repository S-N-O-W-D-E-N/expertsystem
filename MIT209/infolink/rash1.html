Rash

A rash is an area of irritated or swollen skin that often appears as red spots, patches, or bumps. It can be caused by allergies, infections, or skin conditions.

Common Causes:

  • Allergic reactions to food, medication, or plants
  • Skin infections (bacterial, viral, or fungal)
  • Contact dermatitis from irritants
  • Heat or sweat rashes
  • Chronic conditions like eczema or psoriasis

Treatment for Rash:

Treatments vary depending on the cause but often include topical creams, antihistamines, or avoiding irritants. Seek medical advice if a rash persists, spreads, or is accompanied by fever.
