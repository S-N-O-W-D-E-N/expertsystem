Small, Itchy Blisters on Hands or Feet

Small, itchy blisters on hands or feet are often a sign of an allergic reaction, skin irritation, or infection. These blisters can be caused by a variety of factors, and proper care is necessary to prevent complications.

Common Causes:

  • Contact dermatitis (reaction to chemicals or allergens)
  • Hand, foot, and mouth disease (caused by a viral infection)
  • Friction blisters from repeated rubbing
  • Infections like fungal or bacterial infections
  • Allergic reactions to certain materials (e.g., latex or certain plants)

Treatment for Small, Itchy Blisters:

Treatment depends on the underlying cause. Some options include avoiding triggers, applying topical corticosteroids, using antihistamines for itching, and keeping the area clean and dry. See a doctor if blisters worsen or do not improve.
