Swollen Legs or Ankles

Swollen legs or ankles can result from various causes, such as fluid retention, injury, or underlying health conditions like heart, kidney, or liver problems. It is important to determine the cause to treat it effectively.

Common Causes:

  • Fluid retention (edema)
  • Injury or trauma to the legs or ankles
  • Heart disease or poor circulation
  • Kidney disease (causing fluid buildup)
  • Liver disease (affecting fluid balance)
  • Pregnancy (due to increased pressure on blood vessels)
  • Medications that affect fluid retention

Treatment for Swollen Legs or Ankles:

Treatment for swollen legs or ankles depends on the cause. It may involve elevating the legs, using compression stockings, reducing salt intake, taking prescribed medications, or seeking treatment for underlying conditions. A healthcare provider should be consulted for a proper diagnosis.
